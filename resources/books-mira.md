## patterns we've noticed

1. **visual density matters**: books with rich, detailed illustrations where she can find new things each read hold attention longer (here we are, lpbd series)

2. **lifecycle/transformation books**: strong interest in how things grow/change (how do apples grow, very hungry caterpillar, tap the magic tree)

3. **interactive elements**: different type of engagement - more active participation (press here, tap the magic tree)

4. **memorization = mastery = ready for new**: when she pre-says every word, she's ready to move on (lion inside, koala who could)

5. **lpbd pattern**: works when actions are relatable (dancing, painting, sewing) + visual richness. less engaging when abstract (science concepts)

6. **naming = ownership**: she gives books her own names (apple go, kevin, audrey, coco, time, welcome) - sign of strong connection

7. **nature/animals**: consistent draw across all phases

8. **reading ritual matters**: bottle + bed + blanket + massage + book = optimal engagement
